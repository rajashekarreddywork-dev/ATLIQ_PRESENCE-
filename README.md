# ATLIQ Presence – HR Insights & Trends

## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes employee attrition patterns using HR datasets and interactive dashboards. It highlights workforce demographics, job roles, education, age, salary, and years of service to uncover actionable insights for recruiters and HR teams.

---

## 🎯 Project Goals
- **Track attrition trends** across departments and roles.  
- **Identify workforce stability challenges** by age, salary, and education.  
- **Provide recruiters with insights** to improve retention and hiring strategies.  

---

## 📝 Problem Statement
1. Collect and preprocess HR employee data.  
2. Analyze attrition by demographics, salary slabs, and job roles.  
3. Visualize patterns through interactive dashboards.  
4. Deliver insights to guide HR decision-making and recruitment focus.  

---

## 📊 Data Source
- Dataset: **HR_Dataset.csv** (employee demographics, attrition, salary, education, job roles).  
- Includes: age groups, salary slabs, education fields, years of service, and attrition status.  

---

## 🔍 Dashboard Highlights
- **Attrition by Gender** – Male vs Female trends.  
- **Attrition by Age Group** – Key risk segments.  
- **Attrition by Salary Slab** – Salary ranges linked to turnover.  
- **Attrition by Education Field** – Life Sciences, Medical, Marketing, Technical, HR.  
- **Attrition by Job Role** – Sales Executives, Laboratory Technicians, Research Scientists, etc.  
- **Attrition Over Years of Service** – Line chart showing tenure-related attrition.  

---

## 💡 Key Insights
- Higher attrition observed among **Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives**.  
- **Younger employees (18–25)** show notable turnover rates.  
- Majority of attrition occurs in the **lower salary slab (≤5k)**.  
- Education fields like **Life Sciences and Medical** contribute significantly to attrition.  

---

## 🚀 Recommendations
- Strengthen retention programs for **entry-level roles**.  
- Focus on **salary and career growth opportunities** to reduce turnover.  
- Tailor recruitment strategies for **high-risk job roles**.  
- Improve engagement for younger workforce segments.
